Introduction to Contemporary Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning and Deep Learning for Non-Technical Professionals

A Lecture Series

 

About: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is not only bringing forth unprecedented research, innovation and commercialization opportunities, but also providing an entirely new methodology for scientific discovery. Effectively leveraging AI requires a good understanding of its fundamental and development of AI way of thinking. This lecture series aims at introducing AI to non-technical professionals. It will provide the necessary vocabulary and conceptual understanding required to describe and discuss problems and proper use of in AI, including machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL). Furthermore, it will allow participants to appreciate and understand some of the successful AI applications in real-world use cases, as well as the limitations of AI techniques. The series will also provide general information on the commonly used AI tools. 

The lecture series consists of the following four modules:

  • A gentle introduction to contemporary AI and ML
  • A gentle introduction to DL
  • A gentle introduction to Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • A gentle introduction to use and tools of AI, ML and DL

Expected Outcomes: Through the series, participants will have an opportunity to learn how to 

  • Understand the relationship between data engineering, data science and AI
  • Describe supervised, unsupervised and reinforcement learning and their usages
  • Compare AI vs ML and vs Deep Learning (DL)
  • Understand the problems of classification, clustering and regression
  • Discuss how to identify which kinds of problems are applicable for specific use cases
  • Understand training, evaluation, data cleaning, data annotation and data augmentation in ML and DL
  • Discuss ML and DL use cases in different domains
  • Identify when to use or not to use ML or DL
  • Understand usage of commonly used tools, libraries, and platforms
  • Discuss on how ML and DL deals with unstructured data

The series will be delivered interactively by the members of Centre for Artificial Intelligence (CAI), University of Wollongong, in a Dual-Mode (onsite at Wollongong campus and online via Zoom).
